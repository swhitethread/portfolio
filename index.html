SAMANTHA WHITETHREAD

ABOUT

I am Samantha Whitethread, a multidisciplinary maker of things. I am a second year Design student at Goldsmiths University. I have a wide and varied skillset, and pride myself on my ability to adapt to meet a projects needs and requirements. I enjoy speculations, often through materials, and believe that a problem is best solved through making.
I worked as a costume design intern on the project ‘The Human Sensor’ by artist Kasia Molga, commissioned by Invisible Dust, a non profit arts and environmental organisation. My task involved sewing together costumes and masks, preparing them for their performances.

Courgette Crisis

View

A RAPID RESPONSE TO THE COURGETTE CRISIS

Previous Next

In 2017 we experienced a crisis that was set to cause mass panic and unimaginable outrage - the courgette crisis. Due to adverse weather conditions, supermarkets were unable to stock our favourite vegetable. Fortunately, a team of experts (The Courgetteers) took matters into their own hands and devised a machine that was capable of converting gold into courgettes (a far more coveted luxury). The very last courgette in recorded history was cryogenically frozen for historical records. Now 100 years later, we have re-animated the specimen for our fusion testing. Drawing the DNA out and analysing the structure we were able to digitally model the courgette and use specific protein coding sequences, splicing DNA from objects in our historical archive. The core jet systematically analyses the effects of DNA variants on the main steps of gene regulation, from the chromatin state through protein function, to create the perfect courgette.

Unveiled for the first time at the Goldsmiths Pataphysical conference, the CORE-JET 2000 is an exceptional machine that works through a delicate procedure of infusing gold with bio-vegmassness to produce outstanding results.

This was created as a rapid response to the ‘Courgette Crisis’, over the course of a week the matter was investigated and responded to through quick making and rapid prototyping.

Camouflage Escape

ESCAPE THROUGH CAMOUFLAGE

This is an exploration into the use of camouflage as an escape. It is a methodology that has been devised in order to focus on using space as a platform to build a narrative through a performative medium. It is an exploration into using invisibility in one system to highlight and give visibility to another. Through doing this I was questioning what it means to take on the identity of a space, to play with its authority and question its boundaries.

NOWISM

Nowism is an ideology created as an answer to human kinds dependence on and obsession with time. It is a solution to routine and conformity, it is against the image and the icon of the clock. Nowists believe that the clock is a tool designed to control the masses and dictate the structures of society. Its usage deviates from core human abilities; it intervenes with ‘gut feeling’ and applies pressure to those who aren’t ticking along with it.

This project explores these notions and through the creation of ‘Time Trolling’, the idea of disrupting time keeping methods and creating a disconnect within the system.

Batophobia

Batophobia

Batophobia is the specific fear of high objects, such as tall buildings. Batophobia may be related to acrophobia — the fear of high places, or to anablepophobia — the fear of looking up at high places.
